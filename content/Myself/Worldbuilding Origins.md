My current three main worldbuilding projects are: [[Projects/Mysenvar|Mysenvar]], [[Projects/The N'Argonath Experiment|The N'Argonath Experiment]], and [[Projects/Hithered|Hithered]].
- Mysenvar is a low-magic fantasy setting, where readers explore through the [Encyclopedia Mysenvaria](https://mysenvaria.ragno.wiki/) and are free to use the setting as they please. 
- The N'Argonath Experiment is a multi-media mystery set in a high-magic sci-fi setting, exploring the intricacies of different alien civilizations and how they find similarity through their differences. 
- And Hithered is a silly fantasy mish-mash in a world of insects, where a millipede cult has discovered an ancient technology and seek to take over the world.
Each of these three are *drastically* different in tone, style, and planned execution. But all of them share the same roots, and were once all the same project! How!?
# The Beginning
Well, it's a story that starts with a single comic book I wrote when I was in early elementary school. I don't remember the exact details of the comic, but it was drawn and written on a few sheets of ruled paper, and the plot had something to do with aliens invading a planet of stick people. I would show this comic to a friend on the bus—a friend who I wished I remembered the name of—he was a grade higher than me, but we shared a lot of common interests, like dragons, comics, writing, and art. He probably found me annoying sometimes, but I'd like to think we both share fond memories of each other.

Me and this friend would go on to create a series of comics under the title *Stick World* (very original, I know). The comics, amongst other things, included self-inserts of both of us, and importantly introduced the concept of another planet, a 'Stick World', where all the denizens (and the planet itself) are a 2D flat image. The books (as far as I remember) included such thrilling adventures as:
- The stick people arriving to the earth to rescue an ancient artifact;
- A mysterious burrowing creature wrecking havoc on Stick World;
- And violent, *violent*, stick-person death.

Stick World fell into obscurity as I matured, but the groundwork it laid for my creative endeavors never died. As I moved through elementary school and my art got better I started making more mature comics, including a series of *Marvel Comics* parodies. These Marvel parodies included such well-known characters as:
- Spider-Duck (although they resembled a penguin),
- The Iron Man (a man made of literal iron),
- and The Iron Woman (Iron Man's sister, also made of iron).

I'm sure there were more, but similarly to these comics they're lost to time. This era of my comics would introduce a semi-familiar name to anyone who knows The N'Argonath Experiment: Ziptonians. *Well, maybe not too familiar of a name...*

Ziptonians were based off of Gems from the recently released *Cartoon Network* television series *Steven Universe*. I have complicated opinions on *Steven Universe*, but the impact it's had on my creative works is undeniable. Ziptonians were a race of green triangle-headed humanoid aliens who had technology far exceeding humanities own. Among their list of technologies were crystals—which they often embedded into their bodies—that allowed them to summon weapons at will.

Ziptonians served as the primary antagonists of my parody comics, the punching bags for heroes like The Iron Man. They were a long ways away from becoming the Zemynans of the modern day.
# The Age of Magic
I'm not sure how the transition occurred, but late into elementary school I abandoned the old comics and began laying the groundwork for a new series of comics under the name of *Age of Magic*. I still have the *Age of Magic* comics to this day[^1], though some major portions of the first book are missing, and a vast majority of the other books, including an entire bestiary, are also missing.

*Age of Magic* was a massive undertaking, and the first book ended up being 60 pages long (of slightly incoherent storytelling). I look back at it very fondly. The general premise of the series 3 main books was:
- A small mage, by the name of Rage, has his world thrown into turmoil when his wand shatters into 4 shards that he must venture across the world of Magic to collect. Discovering along the way that he's the son of the world's two Gods.
- Rage falls victim to prophecy: when a virus of demonic origin threatens his home world, he must venture to the Tower of Light to vanquish the evil. But tolling the bell calls a threat much more sinister—An Eater of Worlds and the Devourer of the Universe.
- When the balance of his universe is thrown off Rage is called by the Cult of the Horsefish to collect five items just as ancient as the evil they must vanquish.

Age of Magic ended up spiraling out of control with lore. I could write an entire article on Age of Magic's lore, but I wont. Instead, I'll provide a short rundown of the lore here.

Age of Magic was set in a universe where each planet has two primary Gods, often named after trees. These Gods often procreated and had offspring like Rage, less powerful beings who, alongside their parents, were meant to defend their planets from existential threats like Cthulhu and the Devourer of the Universe. When Gods across the universe began passing, like Earth's did, teams of 4-5 warriors from that planet were assigned to protect them.

Outside of the grander setting, Age of Magic's primary planet was never directly named. It was just a fantasy world in the greater setting of Age of Magic, consisting of one supercontinent divided into four provinces, each of which housed its own plethora of developed cities and locations. *It was my first* ***real*** *worldbuilding project.* To this day I have maps of the provinces from different eras[^2], maps of cities with compass roses and rulers[^3], even character sheets[^4].

I worked on Age of Magic well into middle school, even when I moved to virtual schooling. It was a project that carried a lot of weight in my life, and still does. Over time though the setting leaned more into the science-fiction aspects.
# Mysenvarn, the Spec-evo World
By late 2019 Age of Magic's planet had changed into a science-fiction speculative evolution project taking place on a planet called *Mysenvarn*—note the extra *n*. Though the basic layout of Age of Magic's world remained—four major continents—everything else was wiped clean. Mysenvarn was a planet which humanity had discovered, begun documenting the life of, and began colonizing.

This transition in the project was inspired by the likes of the media I was consuming at the time: *Expedition* by Wayne Barlowe, *After Man* by Dougal Dixon, and *All Tomorrows* by C.M. Kosemen.

Mysenvarnian life, at least their animal-adjacent life, were characterized by a prominent lung-adjacent organ called the Caeli Sac, which for the mammal-adjacent animalia was exposed to the elements. There's not a lot documenting this era of Mysenvarn as a setting outside of a few Instagram posts[^5].
